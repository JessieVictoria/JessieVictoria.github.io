OVERVIEW

Pogo Games Revamp

Heads up, this project is under strict NDA, I cannot share any images at this time.

We were given the opportunity to take a look at elder user groups and video games while helping to port Pogo's games from an Flash to HTML5. I identified opportunities for the games to better accomodate the long-tail user-base in areas including accessibility, wayfinding principles rather than memory-based principles, and opportunities for fun mental-building activities within games. I was even given the chance to do a little bit of game-design thinking!

Feel free to visit Pogo and play some games!

The Problem

Pogo.com launched in September of 1999 and has maintained the same user-base for much of that time. The needs of the Pogo users have changed as they have aged, the games have remained the same.

My Role

UX Designer, Visual & Interaction Designer, Game Designer, User Researcher

EA Mobile, Charlottetown PEI

2017 - 2018

STEP 1

Research

Knowing the struggle of elder users with technology I looked at products they already use, like Shoppers Drug Mart's website and perscription programs, as well as benchmark online platforms like Big Fish and Kongregate.
I shared my research in kickoff meetings and via online collaborative documents.

Key Findings

  • Overwhelming feedback and research dictated minimal changes to the UI.
  • This user group will read everything we put in front of them (a first in my experience!).
  • Do not assume prior knowledge, use clear language and never technical jargon.
  • Vision and motor control issues can be countered with UI layouts.
  • Cognitive decline can be countered using intuitive wayfinding techniques.
  • Playing games can ultimately HELP users combat cognitive decline!

STEP 2

Documentation

We were lucky enough to gain access to a good deal of user data as well as actual users. The UX improvement concepts and simplified user personas came together easily. We were able to use these concepts as guiding principles.

Marv

  • Has some understanding of computers, but does not know what do to when things break.
  • Spends his time on the computer playing games and socializing with his friends & family.
  • Is not concerned with leaderboards or competition, plays games for fun.
  • Has arthritis which causes issues during long gaming sessions.

Carol

  • Does not use a computer, prefers the simplicity of the tablet given to her by her kids.
  • Prefers the browser-based platform to the Pogo App because she is familiar with it.
  • Interested in travel and culture, but cannot take part due to her age.
  • Keeps up with all of the Pogo events, earns the Wednesday Badge as often as she can.
  • Low vision and cognitive decline makes new games hard to pick up, so she sticks to what she knows.
